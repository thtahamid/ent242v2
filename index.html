Who are we?02 / 11

Manual grading creates a business problem for universities, not just an inconvenience for instructors.

The customer insight document repeatedly points to grading fatigue, reporting backlog, privacy concerns, and appeal pressure.

Time loss

Handwritten exam grading is slow, repetitive, and concentrated around midterm and final deadlines.

Quality risk

Fatigue increases the risk of inconsistent marks, delayed feedback, and student complaints.

Compliance burden

CLO tracking, ABET-style reports, and course documentation are still assembled manually in many departments.

grading pressure deadlines appeals reports

The pain is not only "grading takes long." It is the chain reaction: overloaded instructors, slower student feedback, appeal friction, and weak reporting evidence.
